About the Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is one of the most iconic and beautiful monuments in the world. It is a symbol of love, built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth in 1631. Construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and was completed in 1653, employing thousands of workers, artisans, and architects.

The Taj Mahal is made of white marble and features stunning Islamic, Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian architectural styles. It is renowned for its breathtaking beauty, symmetry, and intricate details. The mausoleum is surrounded by lush gardens, reflecting pools, and beautiful gateways, making it one of the most visited tourist attractions in the world.

In 1983, the Taj Mahal was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site and has since been recognized as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. The monument continues to attract millions of visitors each year, making it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to India.
